Spaghetti Bolognese Recipe

Spaghetti Bolognese is a traditional Italian pasta dish loved all around the world. It features perfectly cooked spaghetti topped with a rich and flavorful meat sauce made from minced beef, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and a blend of herbs and spices. The slow-cooked sauce gives it a deep, comforting taste that makes every bite unforgettable — a true classic of Italian cuisine!

Ingredients:

  • 400g (14 oz) spaghetti
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 400g (14 oz) minced beef
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stick, finely chopped
  • 400g (14 oz) canned chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

Steps:

  1. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until soft and fragrant.
  3. Add the minced beef and cook until browned, breaking it apart with a spoon as it cooks.
  4. Stir in the chopped carrot and celery, cooking for another 5 minutes.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, and basil. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Reduce the heat and let the sauce simmer gently for 20–30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Combine the cooked spaghetti with the sauce and toss well to coat.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley and grated Parmesan cheese if desired.
